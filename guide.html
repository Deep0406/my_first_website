Rules and Regulations for Tours in India

Before you embark on your journey, familiarize yourself with the essential travel regulations in India.

  • All travelers must carry valid identification proof such as a passport, Aadhar card, or driving license.
  • Respect local customs and traditions; avoid behavior that may be considered disrespectful.
  • Follow environmental guidelines—do not litter, and adhere to eco-tourism norms.
  • Obtain necessary travel permits for restricted areas like certain regions in Ladakh, Sikkim, and Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Adhere to COVID-19 or health-related advisories issued by government authorities.
  • Ensure compliance with safety regulations when using public transport or participating in adventure activities.
  • Respect wildlife protection laws when visiting national parks and reserves.
  • Be aware of photography restrictions in religious and heritage sites.

Do’s and Don’ts While Traveling Across India

Do’s:

  1. Respect local traditions, especially in temples and religious sites.
  2. Use bottled or filtered water to avoid health issues.
  3. Dress modestly when visiting rural or traditional areas.
  4. Learn a few basic phrases in Hindi or regional languages to interact with locals.
  5. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places.
  6. Bargain smartly in local markets, but always be polite.

Don’ts:

  1. Do not photograph people without their consent, especially in rural areas.
  2. Avoid giving money to beggars; support NGOs or local charities instead.
  3. Do not engage in illegal activities such as drug use or wildlife trading.
  4. Avoid eating street food from unhygienic vendors.
  5. Do not disrespect religious sentiments by wearing inappropriate attire in sacred places.
  6. Avoid excessive public displays of affection, as it is frowned upon in many places.

-- By Order From Indian Tourism Development Corporation
